Losing hair by the handful? It’s time to panic! While losing 100 to 150 strands of hair a day is considered somewhat normal, there’s no question that you still get a mini heart attack when you see a bunch crawling across your tiled floor. But that doesn’t necessarily mean that you need help from a hair care professional or to run to a hair studio.

Did you know, the hair cells are the fastest growing cells in your body?

They are also the first ones to be affected if you don’t eat right or suffer from deficiencies because they are not required for survival. Now let’s find the culprits to your hair problems, shall we?

Unlike the labels on the back of our food items, the labels on the back of our hair products like shampoos are often left unread. In the last few years, there has been a lot of buzz going on regarding the presence of sulfate in shampoos. What are sulfates? They’re the reason your shampoo lather the way it does. They clean your scalp and hair, getting rid of all the dirt from it. While doing so, they may end up stripping your hair off of essential oils.

Ever wondered why your eye stings when shampoo runs down the side of your face? Well, Sulphates!

A little tip: If you feel any kind of irritation on the scalp or find your hair drying up over time then try and buy a shampoo that’s sulfate-free.

Deep conditioning

To condition or not to condition, a question every girl asks if she is thinking about stepping out, even if it’s for just a few hours. A deep conditioning session never hurts anybody even if you condition your hair after every wash. Nothing works quite as good as deep conditioning. Squeeze out the extra water after washing your hair with shampoo, and towel dry it. Then take a dollop of conditioner and apply it right to the root of your hair. Use a wide toothed comb to run through your hair so that the conditioner reaches everywhere. Leave in the conditioner for anywhere between 10 minutes to an hour. Rinse thoroughly to enjoy silky smooth hair.

Heat control treatment

Keep away the heat, Vigorous styling in a hair studio and hair treatments including hair loss treatment over the years can cause your hair to fall out. Whether you do the styling yourself or with the help of a hair stylist Singapore, the risks of overstyling is still there. Examples of extreme styling include tight braids, hair weaves or cornrows as well as chemical relaxers to straighten your hair, hot-oil treatments or any kind of harsh chemical or high heat. Since these practices can adversely affect the hair root, your hair might not grow back. Letting your hair air dry and limiting the amount of time the curling iron/ heat-driven products come in contact with your hair to no more than once a week, are simple ways that can make a world of difference in your overall appearance.

Stay happy, as emotional stress can also disrupt the normal cycle of hair growth and cause temporary hair loss. Most people witness a general thinning of hair several months after a physical or emotional shock. Fret not, once the stress is brought under control, normal hair growth is usually restored.
